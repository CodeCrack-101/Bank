Use Strong, Unique Passwords

Use a combination of letters, numbers, and symbols for each online account. Avoid using the same password for multiple services, and consider using a password manager to securely store and manage passwords.

Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA)

Always enable 2FA on your online accounts where available. This adds an extra layer of security by requiring a second form of identification, such as a text message or authentication app, along with your password.

Be Cautious of Phishing Scams

Avoid clicking on links or downloading attachments from suspicious or unsolicited emails, texts, or websites. Phishing attacks are designed to steal personal information, and scammers often impersonate legitimate businesses to trick you.

Regularly Monitor Your Financial Accounts

Frequently check your bank statements, credit card transactions, and online accounts for any unauthorized activities. Report suspicious transactions immediately to your bank or credit card provider.
